<p><p>Ice-cream stabilizers are additives that help maintain the texture and consistency of ice cream during production and storage. Common types of stabilizers include gelatin, guar gum, locust bean gum, xanthan gum, pectin, and other stabilizers. Gelatin is derived from animal collagen and helps prevent ice crystals from forming. Guar gum, locust bean gum, and xanthan gum are plant-based stabilizers that improve texture and thickness. Pectin is a fruit-based stabilizer that helps with moisture retention. Other stabilizers may include carrageenan and agar agar.</p></p>
