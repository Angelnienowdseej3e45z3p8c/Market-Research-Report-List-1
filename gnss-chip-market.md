<p><p>GNSS chips are commonly divided into two categories: High Precision GNSS Chips and Standard Precision GNSS Chips. High Precision GNSS Chips are designed for applications that require extremely accurate positioning, such as surveying and autonomous driving. These chips offer centimeter-level accuracy and are typically more expensive. On the other hand, Standard Precision GNSS Chips provide meter-level accuracy and are suitable for less demanding applications like consumer navigation devices and asset tracking. They are more cost-effective but offer lower accuracy compared to high precision chips.</p></p>
