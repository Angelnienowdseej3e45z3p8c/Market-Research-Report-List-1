<p><p>Below-grade waterproofing can be done using various materials such as bitumen, bentonite, and rubberized asphalt. Bitumen is a common choice due to its durability and flexibility. Bentonite is a natural clay material that swells when wet, forming a barrier against water. Rubberized asphalt combines asphalt with rubber, creating a waterproof membrane that is resistant to cracking. Each type of waterproofing material has its own characteristics and advantages, making them suitable for different below-grade applications.</p></p>
