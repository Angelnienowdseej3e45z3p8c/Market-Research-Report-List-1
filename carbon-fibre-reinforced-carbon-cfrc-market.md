<p><p>Carbon Fibre Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) is a material known for its exceptional strength and lightweight properties, making it ideal for high-performance applications. The two main market types for CFRC production are Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing carbon atoms onto a substrate in a high-temperature environment, while the Liquid Impregnation Process involves infusing carbon fibers with a liquid resin to create a strong composite material. Both processes are crucial in meeting the growing demand for CFRC in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and sports equipment.</p></p>
