<p><p>Glycolipids biosurfactant market is divided into two main types: sophorolipids and rhamnolipids. Sophorolipids are produced by yeast and are known for their high biodegradability and low toxicity, making them popular in various industries including cosmetics and food. Rhamnolipids, on the other hand, are produced by bacteria and are valued for their ability to emulsify oil and enhance the biodegradation of hydrophobic compounds. Both types of biosurfactants are gaining traction due to their environmentally-friendly nature and versatile applications.</p></p>
