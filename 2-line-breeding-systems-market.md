<p><p>Type I market refers to a breeding system where animals are bred within closed herds or flocks, without introducing any outside genetics. This results in a more homogeneous population with limited genetic diversity. Type II market involves animals being bred across different herds or flocks, allowing for the introduction of new genetics. This leads to a more diverse population with potentially higher levels of genetic variability. Both markets have their advantages and drawbacks, depending on the desired breeding goals and outcomes.</p></p>
