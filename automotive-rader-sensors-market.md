<p><p>Automotive radar sensors are commonly categorized into two main types: CMOS and CCD. Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (CMOS) sensors are known for their low power consumption and compact size, making them ideal for automotive applications. Charge-Coupled Device (CCD) sensors are known for their higher image quality and better light sensitivity compared to CMOS sensors. Each type of sensor has its own advantages and is used in different automotive radar applications based on specific requirements such as power consumption, size, image quality, and light sensitivity.</p></p>
