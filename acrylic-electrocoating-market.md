<p><p>Acrylic electrocoating is a process used to apply a protective coating to various surfaces. There are three types of market for acrylic electrocoating: cathodic, anodic, and other. In the cathodic market, the object being coated acts as the cathode (negative electrode), while the paint acts as the anode (positive electrode). This method provides excellent corrosion resistance. In the anodic market, the object being coated acts as the anode, providing an extra level of protection against corrosion. The other market includes variations of the process that may not fit into the cathodic or anodic categories.</p></p>
