<p><p>Non-infectious macular edema treatment market includes various types of therapies such as anti-VEGF therapy, corticosteroids, immune inhibitors, biological preparations, and other approaches. Anti-VEGF therapy involves blocking vascular endothelial growth factor to reduce leakage and swelling in the macula. Corticosteroids work by reducing inflammation and swelling in the eye. Immune inhibitors target the immune response that leads to macular edema. Biological preparations involve using proteins or antibodies to target specific pathways contributing to edema. Other therapies may include laser treatments or surgical interventions.</p></p>
